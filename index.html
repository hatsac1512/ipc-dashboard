Regional IPC Progress

Western Pacific Region, 2025-2030

Infection prevention and control (IPC) is a core health-system function that protects patients, health and care workers, families and communities from healthcare-associated infections (HAIs). Strong IPC also supports antimicrobial resistance containment, universal health coverage, patient safety, quality of care, and health security.

This dashboard presents regional and country-level progress on strengthening IPC in the Western Pacific Region. It is informed by the 2025 Western Pacific Regional IPC Survey, country benchmarking against the WHO Global Action Plan and Monitoring Framework on IPC, and HAI surveillance readiness assessments at national and health-care facility levels.

The dashboard is aligned with key global and regional guidance, including the WHO Global Strategy on IPC, the WHO Global Action Plan and Monitoring Framework on IPC 2024-2030, the WHO Global Report on IPC 2024, the WHO Minimum Requirements for IPC Programmes, and the Asia Pacific Health Security Action Framework. It also supports implementation of the Regional Action Plan on Strengthening IPC in the Western Pacific Region 2025-2030.

What This Dashboard Covers

Data should be interpreted as a tool for learning and improvement, taking into account differences in country contexts, health-system structures, and reporting capacity.

IPC progress across eight Strategic Directions: governance, active IPC programmes, integration and coordination, workforce knowledge and career pathways, data for action, advocacy, research, and partnerships.
Country and regional priorities for 2025-2030.
HAI surveillance readiness at national and health-care facility levels.
Monitoring and evaluation indicators, including 2025 baselines and targets for 2027 and 2030.
Data quality and accountability, supporting evidence-based planning, technical assistance, and regional learning.
