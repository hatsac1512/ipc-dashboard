WHO Minimum Requirements for National HAI Surveillance — Overall & by Income Level
% of countries/areas meeting each WHO minimum requirement, overall and by income group (LMICs n=14, HICs n=9, Total N=23).

WHO defines two core elements as essential for national HAI surveillance: a designated multidisciplinary technical group (IPC professionals, epidemiologists, microbiologists, clinicians and other surveillance stakeholders) and a national HAI surveillance strategic plan developed by that group (objectives, roles, indicators and reporting arrangements).

A technical group was reported by 52.2% of countries/areas (LMICs 57.1% vs HICs 44.4%) and a strategic plan by 39.1% (LMICs 42.9% vs HICs 33.3%); 26.1% reported having both (LMICs 28.6% vs HICs 22.2%) — coverage was consistently higher in LMICs than HICs.
