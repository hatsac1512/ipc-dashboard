Regional IPC Priorities 2025–2030
1
Strengthening governance, political commitment, and IPC leadership
Support Member States to strengthen government stewardship through clear governance arrangements, dedicated IPC leadership roles at national and facility levels, and sustained political and financial commitment. This includes linking IPC to national financing mechanisms across UHC, quality and patient safety, AMR, and emergency preparedness.
2
Closing the gap from guideline to implementation
Strengthen implementation of IPC standards at all care settings, including PHC, by addressing barriers that prevent guidance from being translated into daily practice. Includes practical implementation tools, supportive supervision, mentoring and hands-on training to improve routine IPC performance.
3
Strengthening HAI surveillance, monitoring, accountability, and data quality
Support Member States to develop national and facility HAI surveillance capacity, including feasible surveillance protocols, options to digitalize data systems, and improved data quality. Strengthen monitoring so IPC performance data are collected consistently and linked to accountability at facility, national, and regional levels.
4
Using data for action, outcomes, and evidence generation
Enable facilities and ministries to analyze, interpret, and act on IPC data to drive targeted improvements. Includes improvements in hand hygiene, aseptic technique, environmental cleaning, waste management, and measurable HAI reduction. Provide embedded coaching to convert routine data into usable evidence products such as dashboards and quality improvement reports.
5
Strengthening the IPC workforce capacity and career pathways
Support competency-based workforce development including standardized training, structured career pathways, and professional recognition of IPC professionals. Includes advocacy for dedicated IPC leadership roles, strengthening analytical capacity for data interpretation and quality improvement.
6
Strengthening healthcare waste management and safe climate-resilient environments
Provide technical assistance for healthcare waste management including governance frameworks, safe operational procedures, and workforce training. Support integration of IPC into infrastructure, WASH, and supply systems to strengthen safe service delivery and resilience, including in small island and resource-limited contexts.
7
Fostering a culture of quality improvement, innovation, and regional peer learning
Embed IPC within the broader quality of care agenda by strengthening a culture of safety, continuous improvement, and leadership commitment. Facilitate regional peer learning, cross-country exchange, and scalable digital and innovation-enabled approaches.

Pacific Subregional Alignment
Pacific Infection Control Network (PICNet) Strategic Plan 2025–2030
For the 22 Pacific member states, PICNet's priorities — education, workforce development, advocacy, and data-driven monitoring — reinforce the Regional focus on translating guidance into practice, strengthening leadership and accountability, building career pathways, and using data for measurable outcomes. PICNet provides a subregional mechanism to operationalize the Regional vision and support sustained IPC implementation in Pacific countries.
